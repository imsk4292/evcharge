ROI Calculation for EV Charging Station (Including 7% Interest)

Assumptions

  • Vehicles:
    • Car: Tata Nexon EV with ~30 kWh battery
    • Two-wheeler: ~2 kWh battery (assumed)
  • Charging Price: ₹10 per kWh
  • Electricity Cost (Expense): ₹7 per kWh
  • Profit Margin per kWh: ₹3 per kWh (₹10 - ₹7)
  • Charging Pattern:
    • Weekdays (Mon-Fri): 5 two-wheelers and 2 cars per day
    • Weekends (Sat-Sun): 10 two-wheelers and 5 cars per day
  • Interest Rate on Investment: 7% per annum

Revenue & Margin per Full Charge

Vehicle Type Battery Capacity Revenue per Charge Electricity Cost per Charge Profit per Charge
Car (Tata Nexon EV) 30 kWh 30 kWh × ₹10 = ₹300 30 kWh × ₹7 = ₹210 ₹300 – ₹210 = ₹90
Two-Wheeler 2 kWh 2 kWh × ₹10 = ₹20 2 kWh × ₹7 = ₹14 ₹20 – ₹14 = ₹6

Daily & Weekly Profit Calculations

Weekdays (Monday to Friday)

  • Cars: 2 cars/day → 2 × ₹90 = ₹180 profit/day
  • Two-Wheelers: 5 two-wheelers/day → 5 × ₹6 = ₹30 profit/day
  • Total Profit per Weekday: ₹180 + ₹30 = ₹210
  • For 5 weekdays: 5 × ₹210 = ₹1,050

Weekends (Saturday and Sunday)

  • Cars: 5 cars/day → 5 × ₹90 = ₹450 profit/day
  • Two-Wheelers: 10 two-wheelers/day → 10 × ₹6 = ₹60 profit/day
  • Total Profit per Weekend Day: ₹450 + ₹60 = ₹510
  • For 2 weekend days: 2 × ₹510 = ₹1,020

Total Weekly Profit

Weekdays Profit (Mon-Fri): ₹1,050
Weekends Profit (Sat-Sun): ₹1,020
Total Weekly Profit = ₹1,050 + ₹1,020 = ₹2,070

Monthly & Annual Profit Estimates (Before Interest)

  • Monthly Profit: Approximately 4 weeks × ₹2,070 = ₹8,280
  • Annual Profit: ₹8,280 × 12 ≈ ₹99,360

Impact of 7% Annual Interest on Investment

The annual interest cost is calculated on the initial investment. We’ll look at two investment scenarios:

Investment Amount Annual Interest Cost (7%) Effective Annual Profit
(Annual Profit – Interest Cost)
₹10,00,000 ₹10,00,000 × 7% = ₹70,000 ₹99,360 – ₹70,000 = ₹29,360
₹15,00,000 ₹15,00,000 × 7% = ₹1,05,000 ₹99,360 – ₹1,05,000 = –₹5,640

Revised ROI Analysis

Scenario 1: Investment of ₹10,00,000
With an effective annual profit of ₹29,360, the payback period would be approximately:

Payback Period ≈ ₹10,00,000 / ₹29,360 ≈ 34 years

Scenario 2: Investment of ₹15,00,000
With an effective annual profit of –₹5,640 (i.e., a loss when considering interest costs), the operation would not be profitable under these assumptions.

Notes & Considerations

  • The above calculations assume each charging session fully charges the vehicle’s battery.
  • The profit margin is based on a per kWh basis. Additional costs (maintenance, rent, network fees, manpower, etc.) are not included.
  • This analysis does not consider any potential revenue from supplementary services or government subsidies.
  • Utilization rates, local market conditions, and pricing adjustments can significantly impact the ROI.
  • With the addition of interest costs, achieving a profitable return may require higher utilization rates or alternative revenue streams.

Conclusion

Under the provided assumptions and incorporating a 7% annual interest cost on your investment, the effective annual profit for an investment of ₹10,00,000 drops to ₹29,360, resulting in a payback period of roughly 34 years. An investment of ₹15,00,000 would result in a net loss on an annual basis. These findings highlight the need to either increase utilization, adjust pricing, reduce operational costs, or secure additional revenue sources to improve the investment’s viability.
